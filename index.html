The GSP Approach

Our approach, regardless of the channel or target, is the same: to make stuff people care about.

The digital space offers a rich palette to draw from. It offers freedom to try new things, learn from behaviors, tailor ideas and invite users to be part of our creative journey.

In short, it is a channel we value greatly.

In 2012 we introduced the Beta Group, a cross-discipline department that includes creatives, web developers, designers, user-experience experts and interactive producers - all led by our new chief digital officer. The mission is to drive our agency ideas forward using technology, interactive prototypes and in-house production.

The Beta Group works with our account, strategy and creative teams to build on ideas and help us think about things a little differently.

It's about creating something - a piece of content, a conversation, an insight - that grabs attention, turns on a little light in a person's head and changes behavior.


Case Studies

When we make things people care about, we're ultimately asking them to take an action. GSP has created numerous digital and social experiments for our clients over the years, and we've had a lot of success - and a lot of fun - engaging people and watching them react in any number of forms, from simply sharing to building their own community to making a purchase.

Below are four examples that we're particularly proud of that showcase how we can execute on an idea and how well communities have responded.

Birding the Net|Chevy Game Time App|Dalí GoodPak|Rich's Assistant

National Audubon Society—Birding the Net

For the National Audubon Society, we took an offline, often solitary pastime and turned it into a communal online frenzy that was enjoyed by seasoned ornithologists and a new generation who were experiencing bird watching for the first time ever.



Chevy—Game Time App

Social is often about a shared experience. Chevy leveraged one of America’s largest shared experiences: the Super Bowl. For decades advertisers have flocked to the Super Bowl, but there is one simple secret most advertisers have failed to realize: the majority of Americans watching the Super Bowl don’t care about the game. Chevy hacked one of the biggest social events by creating a second-screen experience that made the Super Bowl truly social. The Chevy Game Time app acted as a Trojan horse, allowing the brand to push even more content to this enormous audience and earning more of the Super Bowl conversation than paid media could ever have bought.



Dalí Museum—GoodPak

Our challenge was to stir international attention for a major museum opening without any paid-media budget to support it, so the agency partnered with Hipstamatic, an inherently sharable mobile application, to create this successful, one-of-a-kind promotion.
